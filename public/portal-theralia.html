      Why and General Overview

      Precision therapeutics begin with biological recognition

      Current therapies often act throughout the body instead of only at the disease site. This limits precision and increases systemic exposure. Theralia is built on a different principle — therapeutic activity should be activated only where it is needed.

      Biological activity can be triggered locally through enzymatic processes
      Nuclease concept — Phase 1 overview

      Disease environments contain specific enzymatic activities that can act as molecular signals. Theralia uses these activities as biomarkers and designs oligonucleotide-based systems that respond selectively to them.

      Take Home Message
      Specific nucleases act as biomarkers of disease, and tailored oligonucleotide substrates enable selective recognition and activation.

      Technology

      Localized therapeutic activation in antimicrobial resistance

      Antimicrobial resistance requires more precise therapeutic strategies that act directly at infection sites while minimizing systemic exposure. Theralia applies its delivery-first approach to selectively activate therapeutics in the local bacterial environment.

      TOUCAN-MRSA: Localized activation at the site of infection
      Conceptual animation of the TOUCAN-MRSA proof of concept, showing localized activation in the infection environment.

      In the TOUCAN-MRSA system, therapeutic activation is triggered by bacterial associated activity at the infection site, enabling localized release of active compounds.

      How it works
      Step 1
      Delivery
      The therapeutic system reaches infected tissue without active effect during circulation.
      Step 2
      Activation
      Local bacterial associated signals trigger selective activation at the infection site.
      Step 3
      Action
      The active compound is released where treatment is needed.

      Strategic Value

      Where antibiotics fall short, precision creates value

      Positioning
      Not all infections are equal
      While standard antibiotics remain effective in routine infections, a subset of cases remains unresolved, requiring prolonged treatment and often leading to poor outcomes.
      These conditions define a high-value clinical space where therapeutic precision becomes essential.
      Entry Indication
      Bone infections as a high-impact starting point
      Theralia targets infections where current therapies are suboptimal. Bone infections represent a clear entry point, characterized by high morbidity, long treatment cycles, and significant healthcare burden.
      Clinical Shift
      From prolonged treatment to localized resolution
      Standard therapies typically require 7–14 days of systemic antibiotic exposure.
      Theralia's approach enables localized activation with an expected treatment cycle of approximately 3 days.
      This shift has the potential to reduce hospitalization time and improve treatment efficiency.
      Economic Impact
      Clinical benefit aligned with healthcare value
      In high-cost clinical settings, shorter treatments and localized activity may reduce hospitalization time, limit systemic exposure, and improve overall efficiency.
      Development Status
      Advancing toward first-in-human trials
      Current Stage
